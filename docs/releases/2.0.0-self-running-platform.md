## Council notes

v2.0 is the milestone the prompt requires: ClawCamp is a real multi-chapter platform, not hardcoded. The static-data retirement is sequenced LAST per the MVP-critique's explicit safety valve — fallbacks (EVENT_EXTRAS, hardcoded cards) are deleted only after the DB paths are observed working in prod, avoiding the big-bang migration risk other roadmaps quietly carried. Self-serve provisioning (Community v2.0) is correctly last because it depends on the entire stack: chapters schema (v1.1), memberships role (v1.4), and the console (v1.4). The Risk PM's a11y/perf/CSP wall is folded in here as EXIT CRITERIA rather than a deferred release, honoring the prompt's instruction — but I adopted both the Growth-critique's and Platform-critique's warning that CSP must be authored AFTER the share/calendar/map/embed surfaces exist so it allowlists them instead of silently killing virality, which is precisely why it lands in the final release. The full observability/backup/privacy baseline (Risk v2.0) and the RLS coverage audit + indexes (Platform v2.0) consolidate here as the provable 'hardened baseline'. Growth's referral flywheel, public leaderboards, K-factor dashboards, and the embeddable cross-origin widget were the most-objected-to items in the council (MVP, Community, and Risk all flagged them as premature, demoralizing, or an un-gated XSS/exfil vector) — they are deliberately NOT numbered into this roadmap and belong in a post-2.0 growth backlog, to be revisited only once there is a real network with real RSVPs and chapters flowing through the DB.
