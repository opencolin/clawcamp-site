What we collect

ClawCamp is a community network. We collect the minimum we need to run events, let people RSVP and follow chapters, and send the emails people asked for. We do not sell data, and we do not run third-party advertising trackers. This page explains exactly what we store, why, how it is protected, how long we keep it, and how to have it deleted.

What we collect

Everything below is provided by you, on a form you chose to submit. We do not buy contact lists or scrape profiles.

We do not store payment information (events are free or handled by an external ticketing provider), and the error-monitoring on this site reports only technical error metadata (a stack trace, the page path, and the browser version) — never your cookies, your login, your email, or anything you typed into a form.

How your data is protected

ClawCamp is a static site backed by a single Supabase Postgres database. The public key shipped in our pages is an anonymous, row-level-security-gated key — it can only do what our database policies explicitly allow. Sensitive tables (the RSVP roster, your contact record, member roles) are not readable by that public key at all; only a signed-in user can read their own data, and only an event's own captain can read that event's roster. The trusted, server-side key that can bypass these policies never ships to your browser. The full posture is documented in our security policy.

How long we keep it (data retention)

Our retention policy for the contacts records and related community data:

  • Active community data — your contact record, profile, follows, and RSVPs are kept while you are an active part of the community, so we can keep running events for you.
  • Inactivity — if you have not opened a ClawCamp email, RSVPed, or signed in for 24 months, we delete or anonymize your contact record in our next routine cleanup.
  • RSVP rosters — kept for up to 12 months after an event (to send the recap and measure attendance), then the personal columns are cleared, leaving only an anonymous attendance count.
  • Newsletter — kept until you unsubscribe; an unsubscribe removes you promptly.

Your choices & opt-outs

  • Email preferences. Signed-in users can turn each email stream (events, newsletter, recaps) on or off from the email-preferences section of the dashboard. Every email we send also carries an unsubscribe link that opts you out without signing in.
  • Member directory is opt-in. Your profile only appears on a chapter's public member directory if you explicitly opt in from the dashboard. It is off by default; turning it off removes you from the directory.
  • Notifications. Chapter-follow and RSVP-reminder emails are governed by the same dashboard preferences, so you control how much we send.

Requesting access or deletion

To see what we hold about you, correct it, or have it deleted, email hello@claw.camp with the subject "Data request". Tell us the email address you used so we can find your records. We will confirm and complete deletion within 30 days. Signed-in users can also self-serve the most common changes — editing or clearing a profile, unfollowing a chapter, and toggling every email stream off — directly from the dashboard.

Questions

Privacy or data questions go to hello@claw.camp. Security issues should follow the responsible-disclosure process in our security policy.
