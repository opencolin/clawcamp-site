Share What You Know

ClawCamp mentors are builders who show up and help others level up. You don't need to be a keynote speaker or an industry expert. If you've built something with AI agents, deployed a model, or figured out a workflow that works — someone at the camp wants to learn from you.

Mentors lead workshops, give demos, sit on panels, or simply pair with attendees during build sessions. You choose the format. We handle the rest.

  • Lead a workshop — teach a skill hands-on, from beginner setup to advanced multi-agent patterns
  • Give a demo — show what you've built and how it works
  • Join a panel — share your perspective on where AI agents are heading
  • Pair with builders — help attendees debug, deploy, or ship during build sessions

Every mentor gets a free VIP pass, recognition on the site, and the chance to connect with the most active AI builder community in the world.

What We Look For

Applications are evaluated on four criteria. These are guidelines, not a scoring rubric — each application is evaluated on its overall merit.

Community Relevance

Does the topic matter to OpenClaw developers and contributors?

Session Specificity

Is there a clear, concrete plan — not just “we'll talk about our platform”?

Speaker Credibility

Does the speaker have the expertise to deliver a strong session?

Open-Source Alignment

Is the project open-source, built on open-source, or contributing to the ecosystem?

How It Works

  1. 1Apply below — tell us your topic, format, and what the audience will take away
  2. 2We review — applications are reviewed on a rolling basis and all applicants receive a response
  3. 3Get scheduled — accepted sessions enter a scheduling queue based on topic diversity, availability, and timing
  4. 4Present — day-to-day coordination happens on Discord. You show up and share.
