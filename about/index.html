About the Network

Builder Camps for the OpenClaw Community

ClawCamp is a global, chapter-based network of AI builder camps. Inspired by a proven open-franchise model — which ran 350+ events across the world — ClawCamp is built on a simple principle: the best way to learn AI is to build with other people, in the same room (or the same call), right now.

We are format-agnostic. A ClawCamp can be a 4-hour unconference, a Saturday hackathon, a hands-on OpenClaw workshop, a startup demo day, or an online livestream event. What makes it a ClawCamp is the culture: hands-on, inclusive, community-led, and open to everyone.

Our Story

ClawCamp started as a simple idea: what if anyone could run a great AI builder event, anywhere in the world, without needing permission from a company or a conference committee?

A proven model showed it was possible. Starting in 2008, an open-franchise community spread to 350+ events across dozens of countries — anyone could register an event, run it their way, and connect it to a global network. We loved that model. We built ClawCamp on it.

The first ClawCamp events happened at Frontier Tower in San Francisco. What started as a scrappy idea to get builders in the same room — learning OpenClaw, building agents, sharing what they'd made — turned into something bigger than expected. Livestreams hit 15,000 concurrent views. Events sold out. Organizers in India, Berlin, and other cities started asking how to run their own.

So we opened it up. ClawCamp is now a global, open network — no single company, no single city, no single format. Just a shared commitment to hands-on, community-first AI education.

Our Values

Hands-on first.
No passive audiences. Every ClawCamp event has something you can actually do — install, build, demo, or ship.
Open by default.
The curriculum is shared. The playbook is shared. The brand is open to anyone who commits to running a quality camp.
Community owned.
ClawCamp is not a product. It is not owned by a sponsor. Local organizers own their events, their revenue, and their relationships.
Quality over quantity.
We would rather have 10 great camps than 100 mediocre ones. Every Camp Director goes through an onboarding process. Every camp follows a quality bar.
Fair compensation.
Anyone doing paid work for ClawCamp earns a minimum of $30/hour. Volunteers are welcome and clearly labeled. Nobody works for equity promises alone.

Our Relationship with OpenClaw

ClawCamp is not owned by the OpenClaw project. We are an independent community that runs events centered on OpenClaw — just as local user groups run events centered on Linux, Python, or Kubernetes without being owned by those projects.

ClawCamp events may use OpenClaw, other open-source AI tools, or any stack the organizer chooses. We are claw-agnostic. The name reflects the spirit, not a product dependency.
